# Savingio AdSense Approval Command

## Command authority

The Savingio chief director controls final approval. Factory and subordinate departments execute assigned work. No department may mark work complete without evidence. Final PASS is issued only after chief-level review of generated files, reports, links, layout, content depth, and factual risk.

## Immediate objective

Make every publicly reachable Savingio article demonstrate that the site is useful, complete, trustworthy, easy to understand, and suitable for advertising.

## Non-negotiable content gate

- Public article body must contain at least 5,000 Korean visible characters excluding navigation, footer, scripts, styles, schema, repeated titles, and boilerplate.
- A title, headings, cards, checklists, or short fragments do not count as a complete article.
- Every article must contain original explanatory paragraphs, not mechanical padding or repeated sentences.
- Information must be technically responsible but written so a first-time reader can understand it.
- Minimum flow: reader problem → immediate answer → required inputs → cause or calculation logic → step-by-step action → realistic cases → cost or money impact where relevant → common mistakes → safety or caution → official verification route → checklist → FAQ → next action.
- No invented figures, legal claims, rates, eligibility rules, medical advice, or official procedures.
- Time-sensitive facts must be reviewed before publication.

## Department assignments

### 1. Factory Orchestration Department
- Select bounded article batches.
- Prevent simultaneous conflicting edits.
- Run departments in the required order.
- Stop publication when any gate fails.
- Preserve Publisher ID LOCK: pub-7605193583747751.

### 2. Research and Evidence Department
- Identify real search intent and reader situation.
- Collect official or primary evidence for claims requiring verification.
- Flag uncertain, changing, medical, legal, tax, benefit, safety, or financial claims.
- Never fill missing evidence with plausible-sounding text.

### 3. Editorial Department
- Rewrite thin articles as complete problem-solving guides.
- Use plain Korean, concrete examples, defined terms, and action order.
- Remove title-only sections, repetitive filler, generic introductions, and duplicated conclusions.
- Keep every article topic-specific rather than inserting interchangeable boilerplate.

### 4. Calculator and Action Department
- Verify every calculator CTA opens the intended calculator.
- Explain inputs, outputs, assumptions, limitations, and next actions.
- Ensure calculator content supports the article rather than replacing its explanation.

### 5. SEO and Structure Department
- Verify title, description, canonical, H1/H2 hierarchy, Article schema, FAQ schema, breadcrumbs, category, and internal links.
- Prevent keyword stuffing and duplicate titles.

### 6. Visual Asset Department
- Verify thumbnail file, article markup, OG image, Twitter image, alt text, and topic relevance.
- Reject placeholder or unrelated images.

### 7. QA Department
- Count visible article characters only.
- Reject articles under 5,000 characters.
- Reject repeated headings, duplicate paragraphs, filler, broken links, missing category, missing thumbnail, wrong calculator, missing official route, and incomplete problem-solving flow.
- Produce machine-readable evidence for every PASS or FAIL.

## Execution order

Factory → Research → Editorial → Calculator/Action → SEO/Structure → Visual Asset → QA → Chief review → Release

## Chief review rule

The chief director reviews only the final article files and department evidence. The chief does not manually perform every subordinate task. A batch is reported PASS only when:

1. all department reports exist,
2. all automated gates pass,
3. sampled live pages match the source,
4. no Publisher ID or AdSense integrity issue is present,
5. content is both expert-level and understandable to a beginner.
